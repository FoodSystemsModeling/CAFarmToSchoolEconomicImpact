# Estimating the Economic Impacts of California’s Farm to School Incubator Grant Program

California has made a substantial investment in farm to school through the California Farm to School Incubator Grant Program (F2S Grant Program). As state leaders consider future investments, understanding how these funds affect participating farms and California’s economy can help inform future program design and implementation.

California has made a substantial investment in farm to school through the California Farm to School Incubator Grant Program (F2S Grant Program). As state leaders consider future investments, understanding how these funds affect participating farms and California’s economy can help inform future program design and implementation.

Using survey data from 2024 Food Producer Grantees, restricted-access 2022 Census of Agriculture data, and regional economic impact modeling, this project:

1.  Examines how participating farms report responding to farm to school incentives.

2.  Assesses how participating farms compare to similar California fruit and vegetable operations.

3.  Estimates regional economic impacts associated with state investment in the F2S Grant Program.

Results from the project will inform policy by:

- Providing evidence on how farm to school incentives influence farm business decisions.
- Improving understanding of the types of operations that benefit from the current program.
- Demonstrating how program spending circulates through California's economy.

This repository contains the issue brief, which provides a high level description of the study and results, detailed methodology, and relevant data. All information is compiled in [Estimating the Economic Impacts of California’s Farm to School Incubator Grant Program](https://foodsystemsmodeling.github.io/CAFarmToSchoolEconomicImpact/).
